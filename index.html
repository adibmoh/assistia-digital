ReviewPro

Reputation audit

ReviewPro helps users search for a business, read available Google rating signals, compare the selected location with nearby competitors and generate a structured management report.

Benchmark

Local context

A rating only becomes useful when it is compared with the local market. A 4.4 rating can be strong in one city and average in another depending on competitor quality and review volume.

Action plan

Practical next steps

The report is designed for owners and managers. It highlights strengths, weaknesses, issues to monitor and a practical thirty-day improvement plan.

How Assist AI helps users

Assist AI is designed to give users both a practical tool and clear educational material. The website explains how reputation analysis works, how to interpret Google reviews, how local benchmarks should be used, and where automated analysis has limits.

Educational content

The guides are written for small and local teams that need to understand online reputation without becoming data specialists. They explain common mistakes, useful metrics and responsible interpretation.

Responsible tool use

ReviewPro can support better decisions, but it should not replace human judgement. The website clearly explains that limited public review data can be incomplete and should be combined with internal customer feedback.

What ReviewPro analyzes

The application uses available public information from Google Places and converts it into a management-friendly report. The goal is not to produce a perfect truth; it is to make reputation signals easier to understand.

  • Google rating and number of reviews for the selected result.
  • Nearby competitors found through the same activity type and city.
  • Average local rating and average local review count for comparison.
  • A reputation score that combines rating, review volume and benchmark position.
  • A report structure with summary, strengths, weaknesses, risks and action priorities.
